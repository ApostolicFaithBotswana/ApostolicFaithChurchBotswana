Our Story

A Heritage of Faith

The Apostolic Faith Church in Botswana is part of a worldwide fellowship united by one Gospel, one doctrine, and one mission ” to proclaim Jesus Christ and prepare hearts for His soon return.

1906

The Beginning of a Worldwide Revival

The roots of the Apostolic Faith Church trace back to the historic revival on Azusa Street in Los Angeles, California. During that revival, countless lives were transformed through salvation, sanctification, and the baptism of the Holy Spirit.

Portland

International Headquarters

Later that same year, Founder Rev. Florence L. Crawford established the Apostolic Faith International Headquarters in Portland, Oregon, USA, where it continues to provide spiritual leadership and doctrinal guidance to churches around the world.

1944

The Gospel Reaches Africa

Through Rev. Timothy G. Oshokoya, the Apostolic Faith Church was officially established in Nigeria, marking the beginning of the work across the African continent.

1955

Southern Africa Opens

Under Rev. Morgan Sengwayo, the Gospel spread into Zimbabwe, laying the spiritual foundation that would eventually reach Botswana.

1968

The Work Begins in Botswana

Rev. Alfred Maseko returned from Zimbabwe after a miraculous healing and spiritual awakening, and began preaching the Gospel in Mopipi and Orapa planting the seed of what would become today's Apostolic Faith Church Botswana.

Today

Continuing the Mission

Today, the Apostolic Faith Church Botswana continues proclaiming the Gospel through worship, prayer, evangelism, Bible teaching, youth ministries, camp meetings, and missionary outreach, while remaining connected to the International Headquarters in Portland, Oregon. Read more about our history

A Worker for God

Raised in a small village in Botswana, I was not brought up in a Christian home, yet God continued to draw me to Himself. As a young man searching for truth, I found the Apostolic Faith Church while working in Jwaneng. During the Easter services of 1983, I surrendered my life to Christ and experienced salvation. In the years that followed, God sanctified me, baptized me with the Holy Spirit, and opened doors for ministry that I never imagined.

Looking back, I can clearly see God's hand guiding every step of my journey, from providing for my family and career to calling me into full-time ministry as Botswana District Superintendent. My prayer is that every person will experience the same saving grace and discover God's purpose for their life.

What We Believe

Sound Doctrine

We uphold a Trinitarian and fundamental Christian faith. We believe in the simple Bible truths that point everyone toward the Kingdom of Heaven, placing vital emphasis on three definite and separate Christian experiences.

Justification (Salvation)

The first work of grace through faith in Jesus Christ, our sins are forgiven and we receive a new life. "Repent, and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins.Acts 2:38

Sanctification

The second definite work of grace a cleansing of the heart from the carnal nature, enabling the believer to live a holy life unto God. "The very God of peace sanctify you wholly." 1 Thess. 5:23

Baptism of the Holy Ghost

The third experiencethe infilling of the Holy Spirit with the initial evidence of speaking in tongues as the Spirit gives utterance, empowering believers for witness and service. Acts 2:4

Water Baptism

We practise baptism by immersion as an outward sign of an inward work of grace, following the example of our Lord Jesus Christ and the pattern of the early church.

The Second Coming of Christ

We believe in the literal, bodily return of our Lord Jesus Christ. This blessed hope is the anchor of our faith and the motivation for our evangelism worldwide. 1 Thess. 4:16-17
