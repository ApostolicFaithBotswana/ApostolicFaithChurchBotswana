How It Began

The Gospel Came to Botswana

One man's desperate search for healing became the spark that lit an entire nation's Gospel fire. The story of the Apostolic Faith Church in Botswana begins in 1968 with Rev. Alfred Maseko.

1968 — The Beginning
Rev. Alfred Maseko — Pioneer & Overseer
The Gospel in Botswana had its beginning in 1968 through Rev. Alfred Maseko. He fell seriously ill while residing in Lobatse and, when all help could not be found locally, travelled to Johannesburg, South Africa seeking treatment. There he met someone whose testimony directed him to Bulawayo, Zimbabwe, where a certain church could pray for him. He went immediately — and was healed, saved, sanctified, and baptized with the Holy Ghost and Fire.
1968 — First Preaching
Mopipi & Orapa — The Gospel Spreads
To Rev. Maseko, the call of God became clear through the Word. When he returned to Botswana he got a job with a construction company which took him to work in Mopipi and Orapa, in the north western part of Botswana. There in 1968, he began to preach the Gospel. God blessed his ministry such that in 1970, the very first Apostolic Faith Church building in Botswana was dedicated in Mopipi by Rev. Morgan Sengwayo.
1973 — Lobatse
National Headquarters Takes Root
Rev. Maseko was transferred back to Lobatse where the Gospel work began in earnest. Services were initially held in his home in Peleng East. As the congregation grew, a small hall was built adjacent to the main house, serving as a regular place of worship and foundational headquarters for nearly two decades. Church branches began opening across the country.
1990 — National HQ Established
Plot 3354, Woodhall 2, Lobatse
In 1990 a plot was secured and a tabernacle built to which all services were then transferred. The new location — Plot 3354, Woodhall 2, P.O. Box 664, Lobatse — became the official Headquarters of the Apostolic Faith Work in Botswana. Rev. Maseko served faithfully as Pioneer and Overseer until 1999.
1999 — New Leadership
Rev. Lordick K. Motshidisi Takes the Torch
Rev. Maseko served faithfully and in 1999 he passed on the torch to Rev. Lordick K. Motshidisi, who has since continued to lead the Apostolic Faith Work in Botswana with diligence and spiritual vision. Rev. Maseko went to be with his Saviour on 26th February 2003. His wife, Sister Amelia M. Maseko, who laboured faithfully alongside him from the very beginning, followed on 9th August 2014.
2004 — National Camp Begins
First Botswana Camp Meeting, Lobatse
The national camp meeting in Botswana started in July 2004 in Lobatse and was officially opened by the then Southern Africa Overseer, Rev. Richard Sibanda. Before then, the only annual camp meeting Botswana brethren attended was the Bulawayo, Zimbabwe camp.
2011 — Gaborone
Camp Ground Relocated to Gaborone
Following the completion of the tabernacle in Gaborone, the camp ground was relocated to Gaborone in 2011. This was preceded by the dedication of the tabernacle by Rev. Timothy Kaibula, District Superintendent of Eastern Africa.

Global Lineage

The Leaders Who Brought the Gospel

From Portland, Oregon to Africa — a chain of faithful servants who carried the torch.

Rev. Florence L. Crawford
Founder & General Overseer · 1906–1936
On Christmas Day 1906, after the Azusa Street revival in Los Angeles, Rev. Crawford moved to Portland, Oregon and officially established the International Headquarters of the Apostolic Faith Church. The Gospel Work has grown worldwide since.
Rev. Timothy G. Oshokoya
First Africa Overseer · Nigeria, 1944–1983
Called by God through an Apostolic Faith tract, Rev. Oshokoya established the church in Africa. In October 1944 the Apostolic Faith Church was officially established in Africa through Nigeria. He faithfully led the Africa Work until 1999.
Rev. Morgan Sengwayo
Overseer for Southern Africa · 1955–1983
After a powerful conversion and healing, Rev. Sengwayo met Rev. Oshokoya in Bulawayo, Zimbabwe and the work expanded to Zimbabwe and Southern Africa — including Botswana. He dedicated the first Apostolic Faith Church in Botswana in Mopipi in 1970.
Rev. Alfred Maseko
Pioneer & Overseer · Botswana, 1968–1999
The father of the Gospel work in Botswana. Healed, saved and baptized with the Holy Ghost in Bulawayo, Zimbabwe, he brought the full Gospel to Botswana in 1968 and faithfully established branches across the nation.
Rev. Lordick K. Motshidisi
Country Overseer · Botswana, 1999–Present
Received the torch from Rev. Maseko in 1999 and has since led the Botswana work with faithfulness and vision. Board Chairman, he reports directly to the District Superintendent of Southern Africa and presides over all national workers and ministers meetings.
Rev. Darrel D. Lee
Superintendent General · Portland, Oregon
The General Overseer of the Apostolic Faith Church worldwide, redesignated Superintendent General. Rev. Lee has personally visited Botswana, including officiating at the 2015 Botswana Camp Meeting, reinforcing the bond between Portland HQ and Botswana.

Board of Trustees

The Governing Body

The governing body of the Apostolic Faith work in Botswana is the Board of Trustees. The Country Overseer reports directly to the District Superintendent of Southern Africa. He presides over all Board meetings, national workers meetings, and the national annual camp meeting. He is assisted by senior ministers who are Board members and in charge of five regions across the country.

Sunday School

"Study to show thyself approved unto God, a workman that needeth not to be ashamed, rightly dividing the words of truth." — 2 Timothy 2:15

For a church concerned with the truth in God's Word, Sunday School has always been one of the most important parts of the Gospel work. A series of Bible Study books from International Headquarters are used worldwide, ensuring all branches learn the same lesson at the same time. Botswana established a national Sunday School office, the incumbent being Brother Motlhabani Kemorwale.

Annual Events

Camp Meetings — A Spiritual Pillar

One of the most outstanding spiritual events in the Apostolic Faith is the annual camp meeting. The first was held in Portland, Oregon in 1907. Since then not a year has passed without one at the International Headquarters. Today many countries hold their own — and Botswana is no different.

The national camp meeting in Botswana started in July 2004 in Lobatse and was officially opened by the then Southern Africa Overseer, Rev. Richard Sibanda. Attendance was large from the onset and increasing every year. The camp meeting motivated brethren within Botswana and abroad to attend — some regularly, others occasionally — experiencing a wonderful time of spiritual refreshment.

Following the completion of the tabernacle in Gaborone, the camp ground relocated to Gaborone in 2011. These camp meetings have been held under a different theme each year. Believers from neighbouring countries and beyond attend, sharing the same Spirit of camp meeting and blessings of God as manifested in Portland, Oregon.

Young People

Youth in the Gospel

God loves the youths — this fact reflects powerfully in the Holy Bible where through generations God mightily used young people to the glory of His name. God does not change and His love is unto eternity.

In Botswana, youths were rarely functioning and youth programmes were not effective and close to non-existence for many years. This was a growing concern to the church. God in His own good time brought in Sister Mavis Mateyu as youth conductor and Sister Lillian Thanke as youth leader in Southern Botswana. Together they took all the young people into a singing programme which triggered so much interest.

The coming in of Brother Moses Zulu as the Youth Pastor enhanced the whole youth programmes nationally. He took them through teachings and made them understand what it means to work for God. Today many youths are diligent workers.

Under the new church structure, Brother Motlhabani Kemorwale was appointed the chairman of the Youth Committee. The Fire of God was burning among the youths, and God, in a wonderful way, used them to revive the whole congregation nationally.

"I started with the Gospel straight where it began in Botswana. I grew up in the Gospel and 'CYC No1 is our Motto' was our song. The Gospel was our life and everything. We would go to the Bulawayo, Zimbabwe camp meeting every year, without fail."

Worship

Ministry of Music

Right from the beginning, music has always been a vitally important part of the Gospel work in the Apostolic Faith Church. The belief that music touches the heart and brings everyone closer to God means all church services feature the piano/organ in the forefront.

In the early days of the Gospel work in Botswana, Rev. Loomatsoga Gaogane learnt Gospel music in Zimbabwe without formal training. His passion for singing for the Lord led him to become the first Conductor and then Director of music in Botswana. Behind him was unprecedented support by brethren including Brother Freedom Sengwayo from Zimbabwe.

In 1999 Brother Lawrence Mwape came to Botswana from Zambia and introduced formal music training. A mini-orchestra of five members started performing in November 2006. Today we have flutes, clarinets, violins, trumpets, trombones, tuba and double bass.

In Botswana, two national concerts are held annually — at Easter and Camp meetings. These inspirational musical concerts have always been crowd-pullers for young and old. Many people have been converted upon listening to the sacred singing by the Gospel choir and orchestra.

What We Believe

A Statement of Bible Doctrine

"We believe in the divine inspiration of the Bible, and endorse all the teachings contained in it. Following is a summary of our basic doctrines."

The Divine Trinity

The Trinity consists of three Persons: God the Father, Jesus Christ the Son, and the Holy Ghost — perfectly united as one.

Matthew 3:16–17 · 1 John 5:7

Justification (Salvation)

The act of God's grace through which we receive forgiveness for sins and stand before God as though we had never sinned.

Romans 5:1 · 2 Corinthians 5:17

Entire Sanctification

The second definite work of grace whereby we are made holy, subsequent to justification — enabling victorious Christian living.

John 17:15–21 · Hebrews 13:12

Baptism of the Holy Ghost

The enduement of power upon the sanctified life, evidenced by speaking in tongues as the Spirit gives utterance.

John 14:16–17 · Acts 2:1–4

Water Baptism

By one immersion "in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost" — an outward sign of an inward work of grace.

Matthew 3:16 · 28:19

Divine Healing

The healing of sickness is provided through the atonement of Christ Jesus our Lord.

James 5:14–16 · 1 Peter 2:24

The Second Coming of Christ

The literal, bodily return of our Lord Jesus Christ — first to catch away His waiting Bride, then to execute judgment upon the ungodly.

Matthew 24:40–44 · 1 Thessalonians 4:15–17

The Lord's Supper

An institution ordained by Jesus so that we might remember His death until He returns.

Matthew 26:26–29 · 1 Corinthians 11:23–26

Marriage

A covenant between one man and one woman that is binding before God for life.

Mark 10:6–12 · Romans 7:1–3
